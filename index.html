The Three Keys of Interpretation

Three distinct but complementary methodologies to uncover the hidden layers of the Torah.

Equidistant Letter Sequences (ELS)

The core modern technique. The Torah text is viewed as a single, continuous string of letters. A computer searches for words by starting at a letter and skipping an equal number of letters. When related terms are found in close proximity, it is presented as a statistically unlikely, and therefore divinely intended, encoded message.

Gematria

An ancient system of Hebrew numerology where each letter is assigned a numerical value. Words or phrases that share the same numerical total are considered to have a hidden conceptual connection. Rabbi Glazerson uses this to support and add depth to findings from Torah codes.

Kabbalah

The tradition of Jewish mysticism provides the spiritual foundation for the work. Kabbalah posits that the Torah is a multi-layered, living document—the blueprint for all creation. This worldview provides the rationale for why hidden codes should exist and be discoverable.
